Aldo Villegas (born July 12, 1978), better known as Bocafloja, poet, spoken word artist and Hip Hop, social communicator Mexico City. Bocafloja began his career in the mid 90's with the Lifestyle (1996-1998) and Microphonk (1999) group. As a soloist since 2000, he presented his demo EP insurrecta Language in 2002. Bocafloja was present with more force at the scene of Hip Mexican Hop with the release of her debut album I think, therefore I am in 2003, followed by the event Jazzyturno in 2004, 2005 personally, manual of otherness in 2007 and I exist; matrix prelude to the feed in 2009. Bocafloja has become one of the most recognized Hip Hop scene in Mexico and Latin America icons. Lyrically, Bocafloja addresses issues such as systemic racism, oppression, colonialism from a critical point of view. Bocafloja is recognized in Mexico as the forerunner in the use of Hip Hop culture as an alternative tool for social and political awareness, a more effective and relevant to marginalized youths in the world mode.

Bocafloja, as many young people in the City of Mexico, had its first contact with the Hip-Hop due to migration between Mexico and the United States, which is very common that several times a year, migrant workers return their communities in Mexico with Hip Hop States.1 United Bocafloja met rap music at an early age, without fully understanding the meaning or culture that was behind. Rap artists in Spanish at that time (late 80's) that might have served as a reference were almost null or nonexistent so musical influences were absolutely linked to the fact rap in English from the United States. The first house parties in the city rap began at the beginning of the 90's, formed by groups of friends who were in the rap a point of convergence. The nascent scene was so small that most of the characters knew each other despite living in diametrically opposite points of the city. In 1995 Bocafloja begins to write his own rhymes, but starts recording until 1996. These first recordings were made of very rudimentary form in makeshift home studios.
